History of Pune

Dating its history back to the 9th century, the city of Pune was initially ruled by the
Yadava Empire of Deogiri. Later, in the year 1317, the Khalji Dynasty overthrew Yadavas and took it under their control of Islamic rule for the next 300 years.
Then, the Khalji Dynasty was overpowered by the Tughlaqs. Pune came into recognition after it was under Maratha rule. Although the Maratha rule started in the early 1600s,
the power kept on shifting between the Mughals, the Bhosale, and the Adil Shahi Dynasty till the 17th century.
In 1625, Shahji Bhosale, who was the father of Shivaji Maharaj (the founder of the Maratha Empire),
began the construction of the Lal Mahal as his wife Jijabai and son were about to move there.
The construction was completed in 1640 and Shivaji Maharaj stayed there for almost a decade. After Shivaji’s death, Peshwas ruled the city and made several advancements to it.
However, in 1761, after the 3rd battle of Panipat, the influence of Peshwas started declining and one fine day, Pune came under the power of the Britishers. Once the Britishers took over their control, they established the administration in this region.
Slowly and steadily, several things were designed in Pune and gradually, it gained recognition from people belonging to different regions from all over the world.

Places to Visit in Pune

In Pune, you’ll find umpteen things to do and see throughout the time to keep yourself occupied all day long.
For history buffs, there are majestic forts like Shaniwar Wada, Aga Khan Palace, Vishrambaug Wada, Lal Mahal, Shivneri Fort, Purandar Fort, and Torna Fort. While nature lovers can take a stroll through lush green gardens, hills, and lakes including Empress Garden, Parvati Hill, OkayamaFriendsGarden,
Taljai Hills, Mulshi Lake, Vetal Tekdi Hill, Bund Garden, Katraj Lake, Pashan Lake, Rajiv Gandhi Zoological Park, and Saras Baug Park to interact with Mother
Nature in the best possible way, devotees can pay heed to many temples such as Dagdusheth Halwai Ganpati Temple, Parvati Hill Temple, Pataleshwar Cave Temple, and Sri Balaji Temple to surround themselves with positivity.
Here you can also visit the Osho Ashram to refresh your mind and uplift your soul. All those who are up for a little adventure can plan to explore the amusement parks of Pune.
A few of the entertainment zones that promise unlimited fun and joy are Appu Ghar, Sentosa Water Park, and Panshet Water Park.
In addition to all this, Pune also features an Amanora Mall where you can eat, shop,
and chill around as much as you want. Entry to the mall is free for people of all ages and it remains open from 11:00 AM to 10:00 PM every day i.e., from Monday to Sunday. Other sightseeing places in Pune that deserve your attention at least once for sure are Raja Dinkar Kelkar Museum, Peacock Bay,
National War Memorial, David Synagogue,
Chaturshringi Temple, Katraj Snake Park, Bhuleshwar Temple, Joshi’s Museum of Miniature Railways, and Mahatma Phule Museum. If interested and have time, you can take some time to explore the nearby tourist places
like Lonavala, and Igatpuri to enjoy your Pune Tour to the fullest.
